    Example 1.2.2. Facial Prototyping.

    A study in Psychonomic Bulletin and Review (Lea, Thomas, Lamkin, and Bell, 2007) presented evidence that “people use facial prototypes when they encounter different names.” Participants were given two faces and asked to identify which one was Tim and which one was Bob. The researchers wrote that their participants “overwhelmingly agreed” on which face belonged to Tim and which face belonged to Bob but did not provide the exact results of their study.
